Learning the basics

Basics & Getting Started

  1. Pick a forgiving car first
    Start with a car that’s relatively neutral and more forgiving under braking and throttle. It helps to learn fundamentals without being punished too harshly.The 2022 M4 GT3 and Aston Martin V8 vantage GT3 are popular choices for Begginers. Player Assist+1

  2. Learn tracks thoroughly

    • Drive them in practice/free sessions.

    • Get to know braking zones, corners, elevation changes.

    • Use visual cues (brake markers, trackside objects) to know when to brake/turn. Spottis+1

    • Do hot‐laps / time attack to push consistency. Sim Racing Setup+2Traxion.GG+2

  3. Use assists wisely
    As you start, ABS, traction control (TC), racing line assist etc. are helpful. But gradually reduce or turn off assists as you improve so you can gain more control and make bigger lap time gains. Player Assist+1

  4. Throttle and braking control

    • Brake in a straight line before corner entry, avoid braking while turning sharply. Smooth release of brake into the corner helps maintain stability. Spottis+2SOLOX+2

    • On exit, be gradual with throttle to avoid wheel spin, rear instability. The exit speed of a corner often determines how fast your lap will be more than how fast you enter. Reddit+2Spottis+2

  5. Weight transfer matters
    Learn how accelerating, braking, steering shift the car’s weight. Proper weight transfer helps with grip, reducing understeer/oversteer. Especially when trail braking or lifting off throttle mid‐corner. Spottis+1
